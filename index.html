Air quality index

An air quality index (AQI) is an approximation of how polluted the air currently is or how polluted it is forecast to become. As air pollution levels rise, so does the AQI, along with the associated public health risk.

Different countries have their own air quality indices, corresponding to different national air quality standards. The AQI is calculated for major air pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act: ground-level ozone, particle pollution (also known as particulate matter), carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. The highest individual pollutant index value becomes the overall AQI value, and determines the associated health concern and cautionary statement.

The AQI is divided into six categories, each represented by a different color. Each category corresponds to a different level of health concern. The AQI scale runs from 0 to 500. The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern.
